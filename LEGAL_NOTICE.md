# Canary Legal Notice

This notice is informational and is not legal advice.

## What Canary Is

Canary is a local-first music library manager and player. It is designed for audio files that users own, created themselves, or are otherwise authorized to store, play, edit, and sync between their own devices.

Canary does not ship with music, album artwork, artist images, playlists, metadata catalogs, or streaming content.

## User Responsibility

Users are responsible for ensuring they have the rights needed for any audio files, artwork, metadata, or other media they import, edit, sync, or play with Canary.

Canary does not grant rights to copyrighted media and should not be used to distribute or share copyrighted works without authorization.

## Local Device Sync

Canary's planned phone sync is intended to copy a user's own local audio files from their desktop to their own phone or other personal devices over a local or trusted connection.

Canary should not be used as:

- a public file-sharing system,
- a cloud locker for unauthorized distribution,
- a tool for sharing libraries with people who do not have rights to the media,
- a DRM circumvention tool.

## Metadata And Artwork

Canary may search third-party metadata sources to help users organize local music libraries. Song titles, artist names, album names, genres, and similar factual metadata are generally lower-risk than artwork, but third-party providers may still impose their own API terms, rate limits, attribution rules, and caching rules.

Album artwork and artist images may be copyrighted. Canary treats fetched artwork as user-requested local library artwork for personal organization. Users should replace or remove artwork if they do not have permission to use it.

Preferred metadata and artwork sources should be official APIs or sources with clear reuse terms. Canary should avoid provider scraping where an official API or documented public endpoint is available.

## YouTube And Streaming Services

Canary should not download audio from YouTube, Spotify, Apple Music, Deezer, or other streaming services unless the user has explicit rights and the provider terms permit it.

If Canary uses a YouTube link, the intended use is metadata assistance only, not audio extraction or DRM bypassing.

## No DRM Circumvention

Canary should not remove, bypass, alter, defeat, or assist in defeating DRM or other access controls on media files or streaming downloads.

## Public Distribution Guidance

Before a public release, Canary should:

- make clear that users must own or be authorized to use imported media,
- avoid shipping any copyrighted media or artwork,
- prefer official metadata APIs and documented endpoints,
- document third-party data sources,
- provide controls to replace or remove downloaded artwork,
- avoid cloud sharing and public library sharing unless legal review supports it.
